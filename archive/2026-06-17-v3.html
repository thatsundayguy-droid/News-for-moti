CFA Prep  ·  Concept of the Day
Today's Concept
Time Value of Money: PV, FV & Annuities

Future Value: FV = PV × (1 + r)ⁿ. Present Value: PV = FV / (1 + r)ⁿ. These are the building blocks of every DCF valuation you will do at BII and every bond pricing question at L1.

Annuity PV: PV = PMT × [1 − (1+r)⁻ⁿ] / r. For a perpetuity: PV = PMT / r. The Gordon Growth Model is a growing perpetuity: PV = D1 / (r − g).

Key distinction — ordinary annuity vs annuity due: Ordinary annuity payments occur at the end of each period; annuity due payments occur at the start. Annuity due PV = Ordinary annuity PV × (1 + r). Examiners exploit this distinction heavily.

Exam trap: When compounding is more frequent than annual (e.g. semi-annual bonds), use the periodic rate (r/m) and total periods (n×m). Using annual r with semi-annual periods is one of the most common L1 calculation errors.

Why It Matters for L1 and Your Valuation Work
  • TVM is the foundation of every topic at L1 — Fixed Income, Equity, Corporate Finance, Derivatives all rest on it; it is guaranteed on the exam
  • Gordon Growth Model (growing perpetuity) is what you use for terminal value in HFC and NBFC DCFs — same formula, same inputs
  • Infrastructure DCF at BII: 15-25 year cash flows discounted at WACC — each period is a TVM calculation; compounding frequency matters for DSCR covenant testing
  • IREDA's 22% loan book growth at Rs 93,069 Cr: to find FV at year 5 assuming same growth rate, FV = 93,069 × (1.22)⁵ — TVM applied to sector sizing
Practice Prompt
  • An HFC disburses Rs 10,000 Cr per year (end of year) for 5 years at a cost of funds of 8%. What is the PV of this annuity stream? What if disbursements occur at the start of each year?
  • Aptus Q4 PAT = Rs 261 Cr. If PAT grows at 20% per year and cost of equity is 14%, what is the terminal value of Aptus PAT as a growing perpetuity?
