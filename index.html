CFA Prep  ·  Concept of the Day
Today's Concept
The DuPont Framework — Extended Decomposition

The 5-factor DuPont decomposition breaks ROE into: Tax Burden × Interest Burden × EBIT Margin × Asset Turnover × Financial Leverage. Each ratio isolates a distinct driver of equity return, allowing precise diagnosis of what is driving or suppressing ROE across peers.

Formula: ROE = (NI/EBT) × (EBT/EBIT) × (EBIT/Revenue) × (Revenue/Assets) × (Assets/Equity)

Exam tip: The tax burden ratio (NI/EBT) rises when effective tax rate falls — counterintuitive phrasing used deliberately in item sets to trap candidates. A higher tax burden ratio means less tax paid as a proportion of pre-tax income.

Why It Matters for L1
  • DuPont appears in Financial Statement Analysis and Equity topics — cross-module concept
  • In peer analysis questions, expect to identify which factor explains an ROE divergence
  • High financial leverage boosting ROE is a red flag in credit analysis — examiners test awareness of quality vs. quantity of ROE
  • NBFC/bank ROE is typically decomposed via Asset Turnover × Spread × Leverage — variant you'll see in item set vignettes
Practice Prompt
  • Company A: ROE 18%, Leverage 2.5x  |  Company B: ROE 18%, Leverage 1.8x — which has higher quality earnings and why?
  • If a firm's tax rate rises from 25% → 30%, does tax burden ratio rise or fall? Explain direction.
