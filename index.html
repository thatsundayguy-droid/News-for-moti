Today's Concept
Credit Analysis: The 5 Cs and Financial Ratios

The 5 Cs of Credit: Capacity (ability to repay — cash flow coverage), Capital (net worth / leverage), Collateral (asset backing), Conditions (macro and industry context), and Character (management quality and track record).

Key ratios tested: Debt/EBITDA (leverage), EBITDA/Interest (coverage), Debt/Capital (capitalisation), and FFO/Debt (cash flow adequacy). For NBFCs and HFCs, the equivalent is NPA ratio, Capital Adequacy Ratio, and Net Interest Margin.

Exam trap: Higher coverage ratio = better credit quality. But candidates confuse direction — EBITDA/Interest rising is good; Interest/EBITDA rising is bad. The ratio's construction determines the direction of the signal.

Why It Matters for L1 and Your Sector Work
  • Credit analysis is tested in Fixed Income and Financial Statement Analysis at L1 — cross-module concept like DuPont
  • For HFC/MFI analysis: GNPA and PCR (provision coverage ratio) map directly to Capacity and Capital in the 5 Cs framework
  • BII's infrastructure debt investments use coverage ratios (DSCR) as covenant triggers — same analytical logic as corporate credit
  • DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) = CFADS / Debt Service — the infrastructure equivalent of EBITDA/Interest; must exceed 1.0x; typically covenanted at 1.1-1.2x
Practice Prompt
  • EBITDA Rs 500 Cr, Interest Rs 80 Cr, Debt Rs 1,200 Cr, Depreciation Rs 60 Cr — calculate Debt/EBITDA, Interest Coverage, and Debt/Capital (assume equity Rs 800 Cr)
  • If a company's DSCR falls below 1.1x covenant, what actions can lenders typically take under a project finance structure?
